Lead a Major Initiative

You've learned rapid acquisition, system-wide debugging, platform thinking, and AI-augmented design. Now lead something big.

Step into technical leadership

üìä What Qualifies as "Major"?

This isn't about building a feature. This is about leading something that would normally take a team 3+ months. Something with stakeholders, dependencies, and organizational impact.

üìù

Feature

1-2 weeks, 1 person, contained scope

üì¶

Project

1-2 months, small team, defined boundaries

üöÄ

Initiative

3+ months, cross-team, organizational impact

üåê

Program

6+ months, multiple initiatives, strategic

Examples of Major Initiatives

  • Platform Migration ‚Äî Moving from one cloud/framework/database to another
  • Architecture Transformation ‚Äî Monolith to microservices, or vice versa
  • Performance Overhaul ‚Äî System-wide latency or cost reduction
  • Security Initiative ‚Äî Zero-trust implementation, compliance achievement
  • Infrastructure Modernization ‚Äî Kubernetes adoption, serverless migration
  • Developer Experience ‚Äî CI/CD transformation, tooling standardization
üí° The Amplified Advantage: With AI, you can take on initiatives that would traditionally require a dedicated team. Your amplified skills let you move at team speed as an individual.

üìã Week 5 Schedule

One week to kickoff a major initiative. Here's how to use your amplified skills:

Day 1
Problem Definition
  • What exactly are we solving?
  • What's the impact of solving it?
  • What's the cost of NOT solving it?
  • Who are the stakeholders?
Day 2
Design (AI-Augmented)
  • Use your Ch4 process for rapid design
  • Generate options, analyze trade-offs
  • Create detailed design doc
Day 3
Socialization
  • Present to key stakeholders
  • Gather feedback
  • Iterate on design based on input
Day 4
Planning
  • Break into workstreams
  • Identify dependencies and blockers
  • Resource requirements
  • Timeline and milestones
Day 5
Kickoff
  • Get official buy-in
  • Assign responsibilities
  • Start execution on Phase 1
Day 6-7
Execute First Phase
  • Demonstrate early progress
  • Validate approach with real work
  • Document learnings

üëë Technical Leadership Skills

Leading an initiative is more than technical work. It requires leadership skills that AI can't do for you:

üéØ Vision Communication

Can you explain WHY this matters in terms stakeholders care about? Business impact, not technical elegance.

ü§ù Stakeholder Management

Who needs to buy in? What are their concerns? How do you address them proactively?

üìä Progress Visibility

How do others know the initiative is on track? Clear milestones, regular updates, visible metrics.

üöß Risk Management

What could go wrong? How are you mitigating? When do you escalate?

üîÑ Adaptation

Plans will change. Can you adjust scope, timeline, or approach while maintaining momentum?

üìù Documentation

Are decisions documented? Can someone else understand the initiative if you're unavailable?

üí° AI + Leadership: AI helps you move faster on the technical work, freeing time for the human work: communication, alignment, and relationship building.
